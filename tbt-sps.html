Navigating Technical Barriers to Trade and Sanitary/Phytosanitary Requirements

Technical Barriers to Trade & Sanitary Measures

TBT (Technical Barriers to Trade) and SPS (Sanitary and Phytosanitary) measures are crucial elements of international trade agreements designed to protect human, animal, and plant health while ensuring fair trade practices. Sri Lanka implements these measures to facilitate exports and protect domestic consumers.

Technical Barriers to Trade (TBT)

TBT measures include technical regulations, standards, and conformity assessment procedures that can affect international trade. They cover aspects such as product quality, safety, labeling, and packaging requirements.

Sanitary & Phytosanitary (SPS)

SPS measures are applied to protect human, animal, or plant life from risks arising from additives, contaminants, toxins, pests, or diseases. They include food safety regulations and quarantine requirements.

Importance of TBT/SPS for Sri Lanka

  • Ensure Sri Lankan exports meet international requirements
  • Protect domestic consumers from unsafe products
  • Prevent the introduction of pests and diseases
  • Facilitate market access for Sri Lankan products
  • Comply with WTO agreements on TBT and SPS
  • Prevent unnecessary technical barriers to trade

National Enquiry Points

Sri Lanka has established National Enquiry Points as required by the WTO TBT and SPS Agreements:

TBT Enquiry Point

Sri Lanka Standards Institution (SLSI)

  • Provides information on technical regulations
  • Accepts comments on proposed regulations
  • Coordinates with relevant agencies

Contact: tbt@spsi.lk
Phone: +94 11 2 675 675

SPS Enquiry Point

Department of Agriculture

  • Provides information on SPS measures
  • Handles notifications on food safety
  • Coordinates plant and animal health matters

Contact: sps@doa.gov.lk
Phone: +94 11 2 876 543

TBT/SPS Notification Process

How Sri Lanka notifies and responds to TBT/SPS measures

Drafting

Regulatory agency prepares draft measure

Notification

Sent to WTO through National Enquiry Point

Circulation

WTO circulates to all member countries

Comments

60-day period for member comments

Implementation

Final measure adopted after review

Impact on Key Export Sectors

Textiles & Apparel

Compliance with chemical restrictions (REACH), labeling requirements, and flammability standards

Agriculture

Meeting pesticide residue limits, phytosanitary requirements, and organic certification standards

Fisheries

Compliance with EU IUU regulations, HACCP requirements, and traceability systems

Tea & Spices

Meeting maximum residue levels, aflatoxin limits, and quality grading standards

Electronics

Compliance with RoHS, WEEE, and electrical safety standards

Ayurveda & Cosmetics

Meeting GMP requirements, ingredient restrictions, and labeling regulations
