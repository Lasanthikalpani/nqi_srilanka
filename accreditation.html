Accreditation Services in Sri Lanka

Understanding Accreditation

Accreditation is the independent evaluation of conformity assessment bodies against recognized standards to carry out specific conformity assessment tasks. It provides formal recognition that a body is competent to perform specific tasks, such as testing, calibration, inspection, and certification.

For businesses, particularly those engaged in international trade, accreditation offers confidence that their products or services meet quality and safety requirements, reducing the need for re-testing or re-certification in different markets. It enhances credibility and facilitates market access.

The Sri Lanka Accreditation Board for Conformity Assessment (SLAB)

The Sri Lanka Accreditation Board for Conformity Assessment (SLAB) is the sole national accreditation body for Sri Lanka. Established under the Sri Lanka Accreditation Board for Conformity Assessment Act, No. 15 of 2005, SLAB operates as an autonomous body under the Ministry of Science, Technology and Research.

Key functions and services of SLAB:

  • Accreditation of Testing and Calibration Laboratories: Accredits laboratories according to ISO/IEC 17025.
  • Accreditation of Medical Testing Laboratories: Accredits medical labs according to ISO 15189.
  • Accreditation of Inspection Bodies: Accredits inspection bodies according to ISO/IEC 17020.
  • Accreditation of Certification Bodies: Accredits management system certification bodies (e.g., ISO 9001, ISO 14001) according to ISO/IEC 17021-1, and product certification bodies according to ISO/IEC 17065.
  • International Recognition: SLAB is a signatory to the Mutual Recognition Arrangement (MRA) of the Asia Pacific Accreditation Cooperation (APAC) and the International Laboratory Accreditation Cooperation (ILAC), which ensures international acceptance of test reports and certificates issued by SLAB accredited bodies.
  • Promoting Quality: Contributes to national quality infrastructure development by ensuring the competence and impartiality of conformity assessment bodies.

Benefits of Accreditation for SMEs

For Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs), using SLAB-accredited services offers significant advantages:

  • Increased Market Access: Facilitates entry into international markets by providing globally recognized conformity assessment.
  • Enhanced Credibility: Builds trust and confidence among customers, regulators, and trading partners.
  • Reduced Risks: Ensures products and services meet specified requirements, minimizing failures and recalls.
  • Improved Efficiency: Helps streamline internal processes by relying on competent testing and inspection.
  • Competitive Advantage: Differentiates businesses that can demonstrate quality and compliance through accredited services.

SMEs are encouraged to utilize accredited testing, inspection, and certification services to demonstrate their commitment to quality and secure their position in competitive markets.
