National Policy Framework for SME Development

Overview

The National Policy Framework for SME Development (2015) and its Action Plan (2016) recognize SMEs as the backbone of the Sri Lankan economy. SMEs represent over 75% of enterprises, employ 45% of the workforce, and contribute 52% to GDP. The framework aims to enhance the business environment and promote high-potential SMEs.

Ten Core Policy Objectives

  1. Promote high-potential, viable and promising sectors.
  2. Adopt the cluster approach for SME value chains.
  3. Encourage high value-added and local resource-based enterprises.
  4. Support export-oriented and import substitution industries.
  5. Develop flagship SME sectors to facilitate job creation.
  6. Relocate industries to reduce urban-rural imbalances.
  7. Transform SMEs from trade-based to production and innovation-based businesses.
  8. Strengthen inclusive economic development through SMEs.
  9. Promote regionally balanced growth.
  10. Enhance resource efficiency, including green technologies.

QI-Related Strategies

  • Modern Technology: Promote technology transfer and diffusion programs.
  • Market Facilitation: Support product development, packaging, branding and quality improvement for competitiveness.
  • R&D and QA:
    • Focus on product quality and preservation research.
    • Institutionalize QA and certification systems for SMEs.
    • Provide lab and testing facilities for international market access.
    • Build capacity in public institutions handling standards, metrology and QA.

Conclusion

This policy framework highlights the crucial role of quality infrastructure (QI) in enabling Sri Lankan SMEs to be competitive globally, emphasizing research, innovation, modern technology, and strong institutional support.
