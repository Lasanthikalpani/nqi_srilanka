SME Development Policy

Empowering Sri Lankan Small and Medium Enterprises through Quality Infrastructure

National SME Development Framework

SMEs form the backbone of Sri Lanka's economy, contributing to 52% of GDP and 45% of employment. The National SME Development Policy focuses on enhancing competitiveness through quality infrastructure adoption, innovation, and market access.

Policy Objectives

  • Enhance SME competitiveness through quality standards adoption
  • Facilitate access to domestic and international markets
  • Promote innovation and technology adoption
  • Develop entrepreneurial skills and quality management capabilities
  • Improve access to finance for quality upgrades
  • Create an enabling regulatory environment

Key Support Programs

Quality Certification Subsidy

Financial assistance for SMEs to obtain ISO 9001, HACCP, and other certifications

Up to 50% subsidy on certification costs

Testing Voucher Scheme

Free or subsidized access to accredited testing laboratories

Annual allocation of testing vouchers

Technology Upgrading Fund

Low-interest loans for purchasing quality control equipment

Interest rates from 4-6%

Export Readiness Program

Training and support for meeting international standards

Market-specific compliance guidance

Quality Mentorship

Industry experts providing guidance on quality improvement

6-month mentorship program

Cluster Development

Support for sector-specific clusters to share quality resources

Focus on regional strengths

Implementation Timeline

Phase 1: Foundation (2023-2024)

Policy approval, institutional setup, awareness programs

Phase 2: Capacity Building (2024-2025)

Training programs, financial support rollout, cluster development

Phase 3: Scaling Up (2026-2027)

Technology adoption, export market penetration, quality certification

Phase 4: Sustainability (2028+)

Continuous improvement, innovation ecosystem, global competitiveness

Benefits for SMEs

  • Improved product quality and consistency
  • Enhanced brand reputation and customer trust
  • Access to new domestic and international markets
  • Reduced production costs through efficiency gains
  • Better compliance with regulatory requirements
  • Increased competitiveness against imports
  • Higher profit margins through value addition
  • Improved access to finance and investment
  • Enhanced skills and knowledge of workforce
  • Strengthened resilience to market changes

About NQI Sri Lanka

The National Quality Infrastructure (NQI) is the backbone for quality assurance in Sri Lanka, providing the technical framework for standards, metrology, accreditation, and conformity assessment services to enhance the competitiveness of Sri Lankan products and services in global markets.
