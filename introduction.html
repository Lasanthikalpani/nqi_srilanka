Quality Infrastructure Framework

The foundation for quality assurance and economic development in Sri Lanka

National Quality Infrastructure

The Quality Infrastructure (QI) is a system comprising the organizations (public and private) together with the policies, relevant legal and regulatory framework, and practices needed to support and enhance the quality, safety and environmental soundness of goods, services and processes.

Core Components of QI

Standardization

Development and implementation of technical standards for products, services and processes

Metrology

Science of measurement and its application in scientific, industrial and legal contexts

Accreditation

Third-party attestation of the competence of conformity assessment bodies

Conformity Assessment

Demonstration that specified requirements relating to a product, process or service are fulfilled

Market Surveillance

Activities carried out to ensure products comply with requirements and do not endanger health, safety or environment

Legal Framework

Regulations, policies and legislation supporting the QI system

Benefits of QI Framework

  • Enhanced product quality and safety for consumers
  • Increased market access for Sri Lankan products
  • Improved competitiveness of local industries
  • Reduced technical barriers to trade
  • Consumer protection from substandard products
  • Environmental protection through compliance
  • Innovation support for new products and services
  • Economic growth through quality exports

Key Institutions in Sri Lanka

  • Sri Lanka Standards Institution (SLSI) - National standards body
  • Measurement Units, Standards & Services Department (MUSSD) - National metrology institute
  • Sri Lanka Accreditation Board (SLAB) - National accreditation body
  • Industrial Technology Institute (ITI) - Testing and calibration services
  • Consumer Affairs Authority (CAA) - Market surveillance
  • Ministry of Industry & Commerce - Policy and coordination
  • National Quality Council - Strategic oversight

QI Development in Sri Lanka

1960s: Foundation Period

Establishment of SLSI (1964) as the national standards body

1970s-80s: Expansion

Development of national standards, introduction of SLS certification mark

1990s: Modernization

Establishment of MUSSD (1995), alignment with international standards

2000s: Institutional Strengthening

Creation of SLAB (2006), increased focus on international recognition

2010-Present: Strategic Development

National Quality Policy (2018), focus on SME support and export competitiveness

About NQI Sri Lanka

The National Quality Infrastructure (NQI) is the backbone for quality assurance in Sri Lanka, providing the technical framework for standards, metrology, accreditation, and conformity assessment services to enhance the competitiveness of Sri Lankan products and services in global markets.
