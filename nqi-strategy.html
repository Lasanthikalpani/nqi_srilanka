National Quality Infrastructure Strategy

Building a robust quality ecosystem for Sri Lanka's economic development

National Quality Policy 2018-2022

The National Quality Policy (NQP) provides a comprehensive framework for developing Sri Lanka's quality infrastructure to enhance national competitiveness, facilitate trade, and protect consumers. The policy was approved by the Cabinet of Ministers in 2018 and guides quality-related initiatives through 2022.

Vision

"To become a nation recognized globally for quality and innovation, achieving sustainable economic development through excellence in products, services, and processes."

Mission

"To establish and maintain a world-class National Quality Infrastructure that supports industry competitiveness, facilitates trade, protects consumers, and promotes sustainable development."

Implementation Timeline

2018: Policy Approval & Institutional Setup

Cabinet approval of NQP, establishment of National Quality Council, development of implementation framework

2019: Capacity Building & Awareness

Training programs for NQI institutions, awareness campaigns for industries, development of sector-specific standards

2020: Infrastructure Enhancement

Upgrading testing facilities, establishing new calibration labs, strengthening legal metrology capabilities

2021: Industry Adoption & Compliance

Implementation of sector-specific quality programs, SME support initiatives, regulatory enforcement

2022: Evaluation & Future Planning

Impact assessment, development of next phase strategy, expansion of international recognition

TBT & SPS Strategies

Technical Barriers to Trade (TBT) and Sanitary and Phytosanitary (SPS) measures are crucial for international trade:

  • TBT Compliance - Aligning technical regulations with international standards
  • SPS Measures - Ensuring food safety and animal/plant health standards
  • Notification System - Establishing WTO TBT/SPS enquiry points
  • Risk Assessment - Scientific basis for technical regulations
  • International Cooperation - Participating in Codex, OIE, IPPC standards development
Learn More About TBT/SPS

Regulatory Functions

Effective regulatory framework for imports and exports:

  • Import Control - Pre-shipment inspection and certification
  • Export Certification - Issuance of certificates of conformity
  • Market Surveillance - Monitoring compliance of products in market
  • Product Registration - Mandatory registration for regulated products
  • Border Control - Coordination with customs for import/export clearance

About NQI Sri Lanka

The National Quality Infrastructure (NQI) is the backbone for quality assurance in Sri Lanka, providing the technical framework for standards, metrology, accreditation, and conformity assessment services to enhance the competitiveness of Sri Lankan products and services in global markets.
