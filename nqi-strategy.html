National Quality Infrastructure (NQI) Strategy for Sri Lanka

Overview and Vision

The NQI Strategy for Sri Lanka, developed by the Sri Lanka Standards Institution (SLSI) in collaboration with UNIDO, outlines a comprehensive plan to strengthen the country's quality infrastructure. Its vision is to make Sri Lanka a recognized provider of quality goods and services in the global market, thereby supporting national economic growth and improving consumer welfare.

The strategy emphasizes the importance of a robust NQI system in facilitating trade, promoting industrial competitiveness, protecting health and safety, and safeguarding the environment.

Key Strategic Pillars and Objectives

The NQI Strategy is built upon several core pillars, each with specific objectives:

1. Strengthening Standardization and Technical Regulations:

  • Developing and implementing national standards aligned with international best practices.
  • Streamlining the process for technical regulations and their enforcement.
  • Promoting the adoption of standards by industry, particularly SMEs.

2. Enhancing Metrology Services:

  • Ensuring traceability of measurements to international standards.
  • Upgrading and expanding metrology laboratories and services.
  • Building capacity in industrial and legal metrology.

3. Improving Conformity Assessment Services:

  • Strengthening testing, inspection, and certification bodies.
  • Promoting mutual recognition agreements (MRAs) for easier market access.
  • Ensuring competency and impartiality of conformity assessment bodies.

4. Developing Accreditation System:

  • Strengthening the Sri Lanka Accreditation Board for Conformity Assessment (SLAB).
  • Expanding the scope of accredited services to meet industry needs.
  • Ensuring international recognition of SLAB's accreditation services.

5. Building a Robust Quality Promotion and Awareness Framework:

  • Raising awareness among consumers, industry, and government about the importance of quality.
  • Promoting quality management systems (QMS) and quality awards.
  • Facilitating training and education in quality-related fields.

Implementation and Monitoring

The strategy outlines a framework for implementation involving various stakeholders, including government agencies, industry associations, academic institutions, and consumer organizations. Regular monitoring and evaluation mechanisms are proposed to track progress and ensure the effective achievement of strategic objectives.

Special focus is placed on supporting SMEs in adopting quality practices, providing them with necessary infrastructure and technical assistance to thrive in competitive markets.
