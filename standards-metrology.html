Standards and Metrology Services in Sri Lanka

The Role of Standards

Standards are fundamental to a robust Quality Infrastructure. They provide guidelines, specifications, and characteristics that products, services, or processes should meet to ensure quality, safety, and compatibility. In Sri Lanka, national standards are developed and promoted to facilitate trade, protect consumers, and enhance industrial efficiency.

Adherence to standards helps businesses reduce costs, improve performance, and gain access to new markets. International standards, such as those from ISO and IEC, are particularly important for global trade and compatibility.

National Standards Body: Sri Lanka Standards Institution (SLSI)

The Sri Lanka Standards Institution (SLSI) is the National Standards Body of Sri Lanka, established under the Bureau of Ceylon Standards Act No. 38 of 1964 and subsequently re-designated by the Sri Lanka Standards Institution Act No. 6 of 1984.

Key functions of SLSI related to standards:

  • Formulation and publication of Sri Lanka Standards (SLS).
  • Promotion of standardization and quality assurance activities.
  • Operating product certification schemes (SLS Mark) and management system certification schemes.
  • Providing testing and calibration services.
  • Acting as the national enquiry point for TBT (Technical Barriers to Trade) and SPS (Sanitary and Phytosanitary) measures.
  • Conducting training and awareness programs on quality and standardization.

SLSI works to align national standards with international ones, supporting local industries to meet global requirements.

The Importance of Metrology

Metrology is the science of measurement and plays a critical role in ensuring accuracy and reliability in trade, industry, science, and daily life. Accurate measurements are essential for fair trade, product quality, health and safety, and scientific research.

In Sri Lanka, the national metrology system ensures that measurements made in the country are traceable to international standards, providing confidence in results.

National Metrology Institute: Measurement Units, Standards & Services Department (MUSSD)

The Measurement Units, Standards & Services Department (MUSSD) is the National Metrology Institute (NMI) of Sri Lanka. It is responsible for establishing, maintaining, and developing national measurement standards.

Key functions of MUSSD:

  • Maintaining the national primary standards of measurement.
  • Providing calibration and testing services to industries, laboratories, and other stakeholders.
  • Ensuring the traceability of measurements throughout the country to the International System of Units (SI).
  • Conducting research and development in metrology.
  • Enforcing legal metrology regulations (e.g., weights and measures in trade).

MUSSD plays a crucial role in supporting the accuracy of measurements in various sectors, contributing to fair trade and quality assurance.
