Service Selection Guide

Identify the service that best matches your requirements. The National Quality Infrastructure (NQI) framework includes:

Testing Services

Product safety, Food quality, Textiles analysis, and Material verification. Essential for export compliance.

Calibration Services

Equipment precision, Instrument accuracy, and Measurement validation. Maintained by MUSSD.

Certification Services

ISO standards, Product compliance, and Management systems. Provided by SLAB-accredited bodies.

Inspection Services

Export/Import compliance, Regulatory checks, and Quality control. Required for international trade.

Accreditation Services

Laboratory recognition, Certification body approval. Managed by SLAB.

Training Programs

Quality management, Standards implementation, Technical skills. Supported by TVEC.

Example: "A food processing SME should select 'Testing' for product safety checks and 'Certification' for ISO 22000 compliance."

Application Process

Follow these steps to apply for quality infrastructure services:

Contact the Organization

Reach out to the relevant body (SLAB, SLSI, MUSSD) using their contact information. Provide details about your SME and service requirements.

Submit Requirements

Provide necessary documents, lab samples, or site details as requested. For certification, prepare your quality management system documentation.

Receive Quote & Timeline

Get cost estimates and processing duration for your service request. Most organizations provide quotes within 3 working days.

On-Site Assessment

For certifications and accreditations, schedule an on-site evaluation. This typically occurs within 2-4 weeks of application.

Key Insight: SMEs can access subsidized services through the Ministry of Industry & Commerce's support programs for quality improvement.
